Research Interests

I studied Physics at the University of Oxford from 2011-2015 gaining an MPhys. I then completed my PhD at the University of Cambridge from 2015-2020. As part of this, I spent a year with the Wellcome Trust Mathematical Genomics and Medicine program. The main body of my thesis work was supervised by James Brenton at the CRUK-Cambridge Institute. I moved to the US and started my postdoctoral studies at the Cleveland Clinic Lerner Institute in early 2021. I am currently studying the topology and dynamics of evolutionary processes under the mentorship of Jacob Scott. My career goals are in group leadership and I am starting to look for faculty positions.

My long-term research interests lie in the development of applications of topology to biological systems.
