Research Interests

My primary research fields are computational biology and cancer informatics. My work spans various dimensions of cancer research, including tumor imaging, survival prediction, and eco-evolutionary models.

Cancer Evolution

Nothing makes sense except in the light of evolution. Hyper-rapid evolution and selection in cancer. Mapping evolution can help us to understand treatment constraints and the potential for control.

Informatics and Topology

Modern biomedical datasets are typically high-dimensional. Topological data analysis can be leveraged to identify the patterns underlying evolutionary trajectories and evolutionary spaces.

Modeling and Game theory

Human diseases are typically bolstered by ecology and interactions. This adds combinatorial complexity to a system's dynamics. I am interested in how this plays a role in complex disease.
